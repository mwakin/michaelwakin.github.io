Michael Wakin

Michael Wakin is a freelance writer and DPhil (PhD) candidate in International Relations at St. Antony's College, Oxford.

Michael has reported from around the world, including Argentina, Lebanon, South Africa, the United Arab Emirates, and the United Kingdom. He has interviewed former National Security Advisor H.R. McMaster, former Secretary of State George Shultz, and president emeritus of the Council on Foreign Relations Richard Haass. For his body of work, Michael was awarded the 2024 Overseas Press Club Foundation Scholar Award.

At Oxford, his research interests include international economics, financial crises, geoeconomic risk, and economic security. He is the Senior Editor for Global Politics at the Oxford Political Review and an Editorial Assistant at Perspectives on Politics, as well as a Postgraduate Fellow at the Rothermere American Institute and Captain of the Oxford's M1 basketball program. Michael also works as an analyst for Aurora Macro Strategies, an independent advisory firm.

Originally from New York City, Michael graduated with a bachelor's degree in economics from Kenyon College, after which he worked as a research analyst for the U.S. Department of the Treasury in Washington, D.C.
